1. Bank Fraud Detection
It involves the use of classification models that identify atypical behaviors within large volumes of transactional data. The system learns historical patterns of behavior and calculates the probability that a new transaction is legitimate or fraudulent. It is based on variables such as frequency, amount, location, history and statistical correlations.

Supervised Learning: Linear & Logistic Regression

Supervised learning uses labeled data to train models that can predict outcomes.
Linear Regression predicts continuous values (like grades or prices), while
Logistic Regression predicts binary outcomes (like purchase or no purchase).
Both are fundamental algorithms in Machine Learning.
