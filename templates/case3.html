1. Bank Fraud Detection

Problem: Detect fraudulent transactions in real-time.

Type: Classification (Supervised Learning)

Input: Transaction amount, location, frequency, user history

Output: Fraud probability (0-100%)

Relevance: Saves banks millions annually and protects customers.

Description: It involves the use of classification models that identify atypical behaviors within large volumes of transactional data. The system learns historical patterns of behavior and calculates the probability that a new transaction is legitimate or fraudulent. It is based on variables such as frequency, amount, location, history and statistical correlations.
